Why Temperature Gradients?

The temperature gradient in dependence of the altitude is a key parameter to determine if a given day is good for paragliding or not i.e., the thermal activity can be good or not. Raw atmosphere data from radio soundings are available from the public meteo services, in this case from Meteoschweiz [1]. Furthermore, the atmosphere parameters are displayed in a so-called "Emagramm". An Emagramm allows to graphically calculate things like temperature gradient or cloud base. But it is hard to read. This webtool is an attempt to display the temperature gradients of the most recent soundings in an easy to read way.

Data

The raw data is available at [2]. In the background, this website calculates and displays the gradients from the raw files. The soundings are recorded at noon and midnight (UTC) and are available shortly after.
